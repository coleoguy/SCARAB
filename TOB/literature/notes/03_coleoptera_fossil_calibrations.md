# Coleoptera Fossil Calibrations for TOB Molecular Dating

**Purpose:** Reference document for treePL penalized-likelihood dating of the TOB phylogeny. Covers fossil deposits, vetted calibration points from the literature, and a recommended starting set.

---

## Section 1: Overview of Fossil Deposits Relevant to Coleoptera Dating

Coleoptera has a rich but stratigraphically uneven fossil record spanning roughly 295 million years. The earliest unequivocal stem beetles are **†Tshekardocoleidae** from the early Permian Meisenheim and Tshekarda formations (Sakmarian–Artinskian, ~295–283 Ma), with Coleopsis archaica from Grügelborn/Saarland (Germany) the oldest described species. These are unambiguous stem-group beetles bearing elytral venation but lacking crown synapomorphies. Associated **†Permocupedidae** extend into the Kungurian–Tatarian (~273–252 Ma). Crown-group suborders first appear in the late Permian Newcastle Coal Measures of Australia (Changhsingian, ~252 Ma), providing the key minimum age for crown Coleoptera.

The **Triassic Madygen Formation** (Ladinian–Carnian, ~242–228 Ma) of Kyrgyzstan yields exceptional beetle diversity — archostematans dominate but early polyphagan lineages are present, making it useful for stem-group calibrations within Archostemata and early Polyphaga. The **Jurassic Karatau Lagerstätte** (Karabastau Fm, Oxfordian–Kimmeridgian, ~163–152 Ma) of Kazakhstan is one of the richest Mesozoic beetle deposits, preserving the earliest reliable records of Anthribidae, Elateridae, and early Curculionoidea.

**Cretaceous Burmese amber** (Hukawng Valley, Myanmar; U-Pb zircon: ~98.8 ± 0.6 Ma, lower Cenomanian) yields hundreds of beetle species across most extant families, providing minimum ages for crown Staphylinidae, Histeridae, Nitidulidae, Cantharidae, and many others. Its use carries an ethical caveat (see Section 3). **Eocene Baltic amber** (~47–34 Ma, Lutetian–Priabonian) provides Cenozoic minimum ages for numerous family- and genus-level crown groups; **Rovno amber** (Ukraine, late Eocene, ~34 Ma) is roughly coeval. **Miocene Dominican amber** (Burdigalian, ~20–16 Ma) calibrates the most recent divergences within living genera but is rarely used for deep nodes.

## Section 3: TOB Recommendation

**Adopt Cai et al. (2022) as the starting calibration set.** It is the only Coleoptera study to apply fully justified fossil calibrations following the Parham et al. (2012) framework, including explicit stratigraphic and synapomorphy-based arguments for each of 57 nodes. It uses soft-bound uniform priors between minimum and soft-maximum, avoiding the arbitrary lognormal distributions of McKenna et al. (2015). MCMCtree with the approximate likelihood is directly compatible with treePL workflows: the minimum-age constraints from Cai et al. translate cleanly into `min` statements in treePL node blocks.

For TOB specifically, supplement Cai et al. with two recent additions. First, the Baca et al. (2021) Adephaga-focused analysis tightened several aquatic and terrestrial adephagan calibrations with UCE data and 23 revised calibrations — cross-check their Gyrinidae and Geadephaga nodes against Cai et al. Second, Beutel et al. (2024) revised interpretations of Tshekardocoleidae and early Permian deposits that affect the maximum-age soft bound on crown Coleoptera; their assessment supports retaining the Mazon Creek soft maximum (~307 Ma) used by Cai et al.

**Controversial calibrations to handle carefully:**

1. **Burmese amber nodes (~98.8 Ma):** Cai et al. use ~15 calibrations from Burmese amber. The ethical embargo controversy (SVP moratorium 2020–2021; see Beavan & Bonhomme 2022, *Commun. Biol.*) and ongoing debate about collection dates mean some journals flag new Burmese amber descriptions. For TOB, existing amber calibrations published before 2017 are uncontroversial; for nodes calibrated only by post-2017 Burmese amber descriptions, treat these as uncertain and run sensitivity analyses with and without them.

2. **Crown Coleoptera root age:** McKenna et al. (2015) estimated ~285 Ma (Permian), Zhang et al. (2018) ~297 Ma, McKenna et al. (2019) ~327 Ma, Cai et al. (2022) 306–322 Ma, and Baca et al. (2021) ~317 Ma. The wide spread reflects different calibration sets and molecular datasets. The Changhsingian minimum (251.9 Ma) is solid; the Carboniferous soft maximum is debated. Use 251.9 Ma minimum / 307 Ma soft maximum following Cai et al., and report sensitivity to root prior.

3. **Curculionoidea crown vs. stem:** Zhang et al. (2018) applied Jurassic Karatau †Obrieniidae and †Anthribidae as crown Curculionoidea calibrations but acknowledged these are more properly stem-group. Cai et al. (2022) corrected this. TOB should use the Cai et al. stem-corrected placement (~152 Ma minimum for stem Curculionoidea, with crown weevils calibrated by Cretaceous records).
