## 1. Cai 2022 Calibration Overview

Cai et al. 2022 used **57 fossil calibrations** spanning 57 nodes across the beetle tree, established following Parham et al. (2012) best-practice criteria. All are soft-bound minimum constraints (minimum age + soft maximum). The age used for Burmese amber across all applicable nodes is **98.17 Ma** (conservative minimum from 98.79 ± 0.62 Ma U-Pb zircon dating; Shi et al. 2012), with the soft maximum typically set at 165 Ma (Daohugou biota).

## 3. Post-Cai 2022 Taxonomic Updates (2023–2026)

### Confirmed reassignments or synonymies affecting Cai calibration fossils

No direct synonymization or reassignment of any of the nine Cai 2022 Burmese calibration fossils has been published 2023–2026 based on literature searches. However:

- **Node 29 / Osoriinae**: Yamamoto et al. 2025 (*Megataxa* 17(2)) described Staphylinoidea from Lower Cretaceous Hkamti amber (~110 Ma), including new Eleusinini (Osoriinae). This predates the Cai calibration by ~12 Ma and is still Burmese amber (lower Kachin). The Hkamti amber is from a different stratigraphic horizon and has no confirmed ethical embargo, but provenance is comparably uncertain.

- **Node 44 / Endomychidae**: A 2022 *Insects* paper (PMC9409230) described the first "Higher Endomychidae" from Burmese amber, corroborating placement but not changing the calibration fossil's position. A 2025 *PalZ* paper on Endomychidae diversity dynamics extends the fossil record into the Cenozoic but does not affect the Cretaceous calibration.

- **Node 31 / Bostrichidae**: *Poinarinius burmaensis* (Legalov 2018) was assigned to Dinoderinae. Multiple new Bostrichidae species have been described from Burmese amber 2023–2025 (Háva & Legalov), expanding the family's Burmese record but not reassigning the calibration specimen.

- **Node 37 / Rhipiphoridae**: *Ripidinelia burmiticola* described 2019 from Burmese amber adds another Ripidiinae but does not affect *Protoripidius* placement. No reassignment found.

- **Node 17 / Lycidae**: Burmolycus was assigned to Dexorinae (Bocak et al. 2019). No subsequent reassignment found. A 2024 paper (Proc. R. Soc. B 2024 doi:10.1098/rspb.2024.1671) described another Cretaceous lampyroid from Burmese amber — relevant to Node 18 context but not Node 17.

### Broader taxonomic cautions

- The *Mesolophocateres pengweii* synonymy with *Burmacateres longicoxa* (Lophocateridae) noted in literature shows active synonymy work in Burmese Cleroidea, but does not touch the nine calibration fossils directly.
- Tihelka has focused on Ommatidae and Myxophaga, not on the nine calibration lineages above.
- The Lucanidae from Burmese amber (2024 paper) is relevant to Scarabaeoidea divergences but calibration Node 21 uses Daohugou Lucanidae, not Burmese amber.

## 5. Ethical Status of Burmese Amber

The relevant ethical timeline for the sensitivity test:

- **2017**: Myanmar military seized control of Hukawng Valley amber mines. Society of Vertebrate Paleontology (SVP) moratorium (April 2020) on specimens **acquired after June 2017**.
- **2022**: SVP revised guidance — moratorium replaced by documentation requirement; separate moratorium on post-February 2021 material (military coup).
- **2023–present**: Publication rate in Burmese amber declining but ongoing. Most amber used in older studies (pre-2017 field seasons) is in Chinese institutional collections predating the conflict escalation.

Of the nine calibration fossils in Cai 2022:
- All nine are in papers published 2015–2019, suggesting pre-2017 collection is likely but not uniformly documented.
- **Poinarinius burmaensis** (Legalov 2018) is the most concerning: the specimen is in a Novosibirsk collection (ISEA) and the collection date is undocumented in the paper.
- The remaining eight are in Chinese institutional collections (NIGP, CNU), mostly consistent with pre-2017 field seasons.
- None of the nine papers explicitly state collection date. Provenance documentation is absent in all cases per SVP 2022 guidelines.

The SVP 2022 guidance does not recommend withdrawal of already-published studies but advises against using specimens without provenance documentation in new analyses. For TOB Phase 5, the sensitivity test approach (with/without Burmese calibrations) is consistent with community best practice.

## 6. Age Uncertainty in Burmese Amber

The 98.79 ± 0.62 Ma date (Shi et al. 2012 Cretaceous Research) is a U-Pb zircon date interpreted as a **maximum age for amber formation** (zircons predate resin production). The true amber age is thus at or younger than 98.79 Ma. An ammonite (*Mortoniceras*, Albian range) found 2 m above an amber bed at Noije Bum suggests the amber may be late Albian rather than earliest Cenomanian — potentially 1–3 Ma older than the U-Pb date. Cai 2022 adopted 98.17 Ma as the conservative minimum across all Burmese nodes.

**For sensitivity testing**: using 98.17 Ma vs. 99.41 Ma (upper end of U-Pb uncertainty) shifts the Burmese-constrained nodes by <1.5 Ma, negligible relative to the credibility intervals on divergence time estimates. The main sensitivity is presence/absence of the constraint, not the exact age.

## 7. Recommended Sensitivity Test Design

### Test A (baseline): Cai 2022 calibrations as implemented
All 57 calibrations including all 9 Burmese amber nodes.

### Test B (Burmese amber excluded): Remove all 9 Burmese nodes
Drop nodes 12, 17, 18, 29, 31, 37, 41, 44, 50. The soft maximum for nodes that used 165 Ma (Daohugou) as their maximum remains unchanged — only the minimum constraint is lost. For nodes where the soft maximum was also Burmese amber dependent (none — all soft maxima are non-Burmese), no change is needed.

**Expected effects of Test B**:
- Nodes 12, 17, 18, 41, 44, 50 lose all Mesozoic calibration; credibility intervals will widen substantially for these clades.
- Node 31 (Crown Bostrichidae): Replace with *Stephanopachys vetus* from Charentes amber at ~98 Ma — near-identical constraint, minimal impact.
- Node 37 (Rhipiphoridae–Mordellidae): Daohugou Jurassic ripiphorid provides an OLDER constraint (165 Ma) if accepted; alternatively use Albian Spanish amber Mordellidae (105 Ma).
- Node 29 (Osoriinae): No clean non-Burmese Cretaceous replacement; node effectively unconstrained from below in Test B.

### Test C (Burmese amber with replacements where available):
- Node 31: substitute Charentes amber *Stephanopachys vetus* (~98 Ma)
- Node 37: substitute Daohugou ripiphorid (165 Ma) pending formal description
- Nodes 12, 17, 18, 29, 41, 44, 50: remove minimum constraint
This is the most scientifically defensible "no Burmese amber" scenario.

### Key prediction
The **biggest calibration gap** if Burmese amber is entirely excluded will be in the **Cucujiform and Elateroid families** (Silvanidae, Endomychidae, Lycidae, Psephenidae, Lampyridae) that have no pre-Eocene non-Burmese fossil record. These nodes will shift dramatically older (unconstrained minimums) or collapse onto broad prior distributions. The total effect depends on whether neighbouring calibrated nodes can provide indirect constraints through the tree topology.
