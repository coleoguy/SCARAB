## 9. Priority Gaps for Targeted Specimen/Genome Acquisition

Ranked by phylogenetic consequence:

1. **Myxophaga (all 4 families)** — suborder-level absence; essential for Coleoptera rooting. *Hydroscaphidae* and *Sphaeriusidae* are tiny beetles with very small genomes; genomics effort is low.
2. **Derodontidae** — sole family of Derodontoidea; important for Polyphaga internal backbone.
3. **Lymexylidae** — sole family of Lymexyloidea; isolated early-branching Polyphaga lineage.
4. **Dryophthoridae** — major economically important weevil family (~1,200 spp incl. grain weevils); surprising absence.
5. **Gyrinidae** — the only whirligig beetle family; distinct Adephagan lineage with unique biology.
6. **Erotylidae** — speciose Cucujoidea (~3,500 spp); no representation despite likely available assemblies.
7. **Cupedidae** — only 1 contig-level assembly (*Priacma*); Archostemata is the earliest-diverging beetle lineage; needs upgrade.
8. **Trogidae** — Scarabaeoidea; ecologically distinct (keratin feeders); no representation.
9. **Platypodidae / Dryophthoridae** — ambrosia / bark weevils; important ecological and economic groups.
10. **Gyrinidae, Haliplidae, Noteridae** — fill Adephaga gaps beyond Carabidae + Dytiscidae.
