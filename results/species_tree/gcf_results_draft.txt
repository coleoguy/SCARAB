Gene Concordance Factor Results
================================

Gene concordance factors (gCF) were computed for all 474 internal branches
of the 478-taxon wASTRAL species tree using 1,284 individual gene trees.

Distribution of gCF values:
  Mean gCF: 36.7% (SD = 24.4%)
  Median gCF: 33.1%
  Range: 0.5% - 93.4%

  gCF >= 50% (majority gene tree support): 149 branches (31.4%)
  gCF >= 33% (plurality support):          237 branches (50.0%)
  gCF 20-33%:                               82 branches (17.3%)
  gCF 10-20%:                               79 branches (16.7%)
  gCF < 10% (essentially unresolved):       76 branches (16.0%)

Quintiles:
  20th percentile: 12.2%
  40th percentile: 24.1%
  60th percentile: 41.5%
  80th percentile: 61.6%

Discordance pattern:
  Mean gDF1: 5.7%
  Mean gDF2: 5.9%
  Mean gDFP (polyphyly/gene tree noise): 51.7%

  The gene discordance fractions gDF1 and gDF2 were highly symmetric
  (means 5.7% vs 5.9%), consistent with incomplete lineage sorting (ILS)
  as the dominant source of gene tree discordance. Only 2 of 470 branches
  with >100 decisive gene trees (0.4%) showed strongly asymmetric
  discordance (|gDF1 - gDF2| > 10 percentage points and ratio > 2x),
  suggesting introgression is not a major confounding factor in this dataset.

  The high polyphyly fraction (mean 51.7%) reflects the limited
  phylogenetic signal in individual BUSCO protein alignments for resolving
  deep divergences (~250 My). This is expected: individual loci of ~400-800
  amino acids contain insufficient signal to resolve all 474 internal branches
  of a 478-taxon tree, resulting in gene tree estimation error that manifests
  as polyphyly rather than a specific alternative topology.

Interpretation:
  The 149 branches with gCF >= 50% represent clades where a clear majority
  of gene trees agree — these are robust regardless of analytical method.
  The 76 branches with gCF < 10% are nodes where gene trees provide
  essentially no resolution, and where the species tree topology is driven
  primarily by the quartet weighting in wASTRAL. For these nodes, site
  concordance factors (sCF) computed from the full alignment will provide
  more informative support values, as they leverage site-level signal that
  individual gene trees cannot resolve.
